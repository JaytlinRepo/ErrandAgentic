## Midtown and Downtown Parking
Midtown and downtown Atlanta require budgeting deck navigation and payment app time. Street parking searches can dominate short errands and add 10 to 20 minutes. Use parking apps like SpotHero or ParkWhiz to pre-book for intown stops. Never assume free parking exists for intown errands.

## Bulk Pickup Logistics
Bulk pickups like furniture and appliances require stops with loading zones or dock access. Prioritize these stops before tight intown legs where loading zone access is limited. Confirm loading zone availability before arriving. Bring furniture pads and tie-downs if transporting large items.

## Medical Campus Parking
Major hospital campuses in Atlanta add 15 or more minutes for first-time visitors due to parking structure navigation and internal wayfinding. Emory, Piedmont Midtown, and Northside Hospital all have complex campuses. Pre-download campus maps and identify the correct parking deck for your specific building before leaving home.

## Strip Center Parking
Suburban strip centers and power centers have abundant free parking with simple navigation. These are the lowest friction errand stops in the Atlanta area. Sequence multiple strip center stops together for efficiency. Avoid mixing strip center stops with intown stops in the same leg.

## Mall Parking Strategy
Mall parking efficiency depends on which anchor store is closest to your errand. Entering from the anchor nearest your destination saves 5 to 10 minutes of walking. On busy Saturdays arrive before 11am to avoid the best spots filling. Covered parking decks at Perimeter Mall and Cumberland fill fastest on weekends.

## Drive-Through Efficiency
Drive-through options at pharmacies, banks, and fast food save parking and walking time. CVS, Walgreens, and many Walmart pharmacies have drive-through windows. Bank drive-throughs handle most routine transactions. Sequence drive-through stops at natural route transitions to minimize detour time.

## Curbside Pickup Timing
Curbside pickup requires arriving within a specific window, usually 15 to 30 minutes after notification. Late arrival can result in order being returned to store inventory. Treat curbside pickup windows as hard time constraints in sequencing. Have the app ready and order number accessible before pulling into the curbside zone.

## EV Charging Considerations
EV drivers should identify charging locations along the errand route proactively. Whole Foods, Target, Walmart, and many malls have Level 2 chargers. Sequence longer stops at locations with charging to maximize charge time. Do not plan charging as a dedicated stop unless necessary.

## ADA and Accessibility
Accessible parking and entrance proximity varies significantly by location. Medical campuses and older malls often have long distances from accessible parking to destinations. Users with mobility considerations should verify accessible parking proximity before committing to a stop sequence.

## Weather Impact on Parking
Heavy rain in Atlanta significantly increases time spent in parking lots and walking. During rain events add 5 to 10 minutes per stop for covered parking navigation or umbrella management. Prioritize stops with covered parking or covered walkways during rain forecast days.
