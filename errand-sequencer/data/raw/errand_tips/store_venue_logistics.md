## Perimeter Mall and Cumberland Area
Perimeter Mall and Cumberland area have large surface lots with internal circulation. Good candidate to combine with nearby big-box strips on Cobb Pkwy if hours align. Allow extra time for parking lot navigation especially on weekends.

## Atlantic Station
Atlantic Station mixes garage parking with foot traffic. Allow extra walk and elevator time versus a suburban strip center. Not ideal for quick stops. Best combined with other Midtown errands to justify parking time.

## Mall Anchor Store Proximity
Mall anchor stores may have different entrance proximity to your next stop. The nearest external lot to one anchor may be far from another. Check which entrance minimizes walk time before committing to a parking spot.

## Curbside vs In-Store Pickup
Curbside pickup slots and in-store pickup desks are different operational paths. Curbside has arrival windows that must be respected. In-store pickup can usually flex 30 to 60 minutes. Sequencing should respect curbside windows as hard time constraints.

## Power Centers
Suburban power centers along Barrett Pkwy, Mansell Rd, and Peachtree Industrial Blvd are usually faster in and out than intown equivalents. Useful for packing many short stops efficiently. Parking is abundant and internal circulation is simple.

## Ponce City Market and BeltLine Adjacent
Ponce City Market and BeltLine adjacent stops have walking distance from parked car that often exceeds expectations. Sequence with fewer must-arrive-exactly-at stops. Allow 10 to 15 minutes extra for parking and walking versus what Maps estimates.

## Medical Campuses
Major hospital campuses like Emory, Piedmont, and Northside have parking and internal wayfinding that adds 15 or more minutes for first-time visitors. Never sequence a medical campus stop immediately before a time-critical errand. Always verify parking options in advance.

## Warehouse Club Internal Flow
Costco and Sam's Club have long internal paths from entrance to checkout to exit. Cart return and car loading add time. The experience is fundamentally different from a grocery store. Never estimate a warehouse club trip at under 45 minutes.

## Big Box Store Service Desks
Best Buy, Home Depot, and Target service desks often have independent queues from main checkout. Returns and pickups at service desks can be faster or slower than shopping depending on time of day. Weekday mornings are consistently fastest.

## Drive-Through Options
Pharmacies, banks, and fast food with drive-through options save significant time versus in-store. Sequence drive-through stops at transitions between other errands rather than as dedicated stops. A drive-through pharmacy pickup mid-route adds minimal time.
