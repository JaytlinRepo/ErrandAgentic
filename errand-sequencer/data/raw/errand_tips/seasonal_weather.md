## Atlanta Summer Heat
Atlanta summer heat makes cold chain management critical. Car interior temperatures exceed 130 degrees within minutes of parking in direct sun. Grocery and frozen food stops must be last before heading home. Never leave perishables in a hot car during a multi-stop summer errand run. Budget extra time for loading cold items quickly.

## Winter Ice Events
Atlanta elevated bridges and shaded ramps freeze before surface roads during ice events. I-285 overpasses, I-75 and I-85 flyovers, and shaded connector ramps are highest risk. When icing is possible consolidate to fewer stops and prefer routes on surface streets over elevated highways. Defer specifics to live weather tool at query time.

## Heavy Rain
Atlanta freeways see significantly longer incident delays during heavy rain. The I-285 and I-75/I-85 connector are particularly prone to rain-related slowdowns. Add 15 to 30 minute buffers between hard appointments on days with heavy rain forecast. Defer to live weather tool for current conditions.

## Pollen Season
Atlanta pollen season runs roughly February through May with tree pollen peaking in March and April. Users sensitive to pollen may want to include car wash stops or pharmacy stops for allergy medication as part of their errand planning. These are personal preference additions to the sequence.

## Holiday Retail Weeks
Extended store hours during Thanksgiving week, Christmas, and back to school season change feasible stop counts. Stores are open later but in-store lines are significantly longer. Budget more time per stop and fewer total stops during holiday retail weeks. Confirm extended hours with live Maps data.

## Spring Break and Summer School Calendar
Spring break and summer significantly reduce OTP school-zone traffic in Cobb, Gwinnett, and Forsyth counties. Weekday morning errands in these areas are faster during school breaks. Early dismissal days during the school year create afternoon traffic spikes near school clusters from 2pm to 4pm.

## Post-Holiday January
January is consistently the least congested month in Atlanta for errands. Traffic is lighter, stores are less crowded, and parking is abundant. This is the easiest month for multi-stop errand runs. Return lines at big box stores peak the first two weeks of January.

## Summer Construction Season
Atlanta road construction peaks in summer months. Major highway projects on I-285, I-75, and I-85 create chronic bottleneck patterns that persist for months. Always defer to live Maps data for current construction impacts. Static knowledge can name historically affected corridors but cannot claim current conditions.

## Football Season Effects
College football Saturdays in fall affect Georgia Tech area and broader Midtown traffic when Georgia Tech plays at home. NFL Sundays affect the Mercedes-Benz Stadium corridor. Plan intown stops away from game days or before noon on game days when possible.

## New Year Period
The week between Christmas and New Year sees high return traffic, busy restaurants, and moderate road congestion as many people are off work but running errands. Stores may have reduced staff. Budget extra time per stop during this period.
