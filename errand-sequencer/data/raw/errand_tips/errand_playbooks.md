## Pharmacy Stops
Pharmacy with a controlled-substance or same-day fill often needs extra counter time. Do not sandwich it between two hard time windows without buffer. Always confirm hours with live Maps data. Budget at least 20-30 minutes for a pharmacy stop including wait time.

## Returns and Exchanges
Returns with receipt lookup at big-box stores vary by line depth. Sequence returns before heavy shopping so you are not carrying rejected items through the store. Returns are typically faster before noon on weekdays.

## Grocery and Cold Chain
Grocery and hot food runs work better if frozen and refrigerated items are last before heading home. This is especially critical during Atlanta summers where car interior temperatures can exceed 130 degrees. Never do a grocery run mid-errand if you have more than 20 minutes of driving remaining.

## Appointment Services
Appointment services like salon, vet, and service desks should anchor the entire route. Build travel buffers using OTP afternoon slowdowns. GA-400 south between 4pm and 7pm adds significant time. Treat appointments as fixed points and sequence everything else around them.

## Donation and Storage Trips
Multi-stop donation plus storage unit plus home trips need vehicle load order planning. Empty the car before filling it again. Sequence donation dropoffs before any pickup stops so the vehicle has capacity.

## Warehouse Club Trips
Costco and Sam's Club trips need 45 to 75 minutes all-in including parking, shopping, and cart unload. Never treat warehouse clubs as quick stops. Schedule them early in the day before crowds build. Pair with nearby stops on the same corridor to justify the time investment.

## Government Errands
DMV, county tag offices, and post offices should always be sequenced first. They have the strictest hours, longest unpredictable waits, and often require appointments. A government errand that gets missed means rescheduling days out. Treat them as the highest priority anchor in any errand sequence.

## Multi-Stop Dependency Rules
Some errands have dependencies. Pick up prescription before returning to pharmacy for consult. Get receipt photo before store visit for price match. Drop donation before storage unit if vehicle space is limited. Always map dependencies before optimizing for distance.
