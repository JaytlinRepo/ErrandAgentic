## OTP vs ITP Distinction
OTP means Outside the Perimeter (I-285). ITP means Inside the Perimeter. These two zones have fundamentally different traffic, parking, and errand logistics. Never plan an OTP and ITP heavy day without explicitly accounting for the perimeter crossing cost.

## OTP North Corridor
OTP north includes Roswell, Alpharetta, Milton, Cumming, and Johns Creek along the GA-400 corridor. Stops in this zone cluster naturally. Mixing OTP north stops with ITP or OTP west stops in one trip requires careful sequencing around GA-400 and I-285 interchange timing.

## OTP East Corridor
OTP east includes Tucker, Stone Mountain, Snellville, and Lawrenceville along I-20 east and US-78. This corridor has its own distinct traffic patterns separate from Midtown. Do not assume Atlanta-wide traffic generalizations apply here.

## Cobb County Submarket
Cobb County includes Marietta, Kennesaw, Smyrna, and Austell. The I-75 north and I-575 corridors serve this zone. Cobb stops cluster well together. Combining Cobb stops with Fulton or DeKalb stops requires I-285 crossing planning.

## Gwinnett County Submarket
Gwinnett includes Duluth, Lawrenceville, Suwanee, and Buford. I-85 north serves this corridor. Gwinnett stops cluster naturally but combining them with ITP or Cobb stops creates long cross-metro trips that should be avoided without strong justification.

## DeKalb County Submarket
DeKalb includes Decatur, Tucker, Chamblee, and Doraville. The I-285 east side and US-78 serve this zone. Decatur and Oakhurst have intown character with ITP parking constraints. Tucker and Chamblee behave more like OTP in terms of parking and traffic flow.

## Fulton County Intown
Intown Fulton includes Midtown, Buckhead, West Midtown, and East Atlanta. Dense parking, walkable in spots, but car-centric errands face significant friction. Best sequenced as a dedicated intown day rather than mixed with OTP stops.

## Airport Corridor
The ATL airport corridor along I-85 south and I-285 south is its own zone. College Park, East Point, and Forest Park stops near the airport experience distinct traffic from both freight activity and airport operations. Avoid this corridor during morning and evening peaks.

## Cross-Metro Trip Planning
Multi-county days benefit from a cluster-first approach. Complete all stops in one county or submarket before moving to the next. The most common sequencing mistake is zigzagging across submarkets based on alphabetical or list order rather than geography.

## Straight Line vs Drive Distance
Atlanta's geography means straight-line map distance is often misleading. OTP north to OTP east may look close on a map but require crossing I-285 twice. Always use Drive Distance and travel time rather than straight-line distance for sequencing decisions.
