## I-285 Top End
I-285 top end between GA-400 and I-85 is consistently the worst stretch on weekday afternoons. Crossing it for one quick stop is a common sequencing mistake. Avoid scheduling stops that require crossing this corridor between 3pm and 7pm on weekdays.

## GA-400 South
GA-400 southbound between Alpharetta and the I-285 interchange adds significant delay between 4pm and 7pm on weekdays. OTP north stops in Roswell, Alpharetta, and Cumming should be completed before 3pm or after 7pm when possible.

## Braves Game Days
Braves game days spike traffic near Truist Park and Cumberland. Avoid Cobb to Midtown round trips on game evenings. Check the Braves schedule before sequencing stops in the Cumberland corridor on weekday evenings and weekend afternoons.

## Downtown and Midtown Events
Mercedes-Benz Stadium, State Farm Arena, and Fox Theatre events affect the connector and I-75/I-85 split significantly. Sequence intown stops outside event ingress and egress peaks. Major events can add 30 to 45 minutes to normally quick intown trips.

## Lenox and Buckhead
Lenox Square, Phipps Plaza, and Buckhead Village on Saturday afternoons combine mall traffic with surface street congestion. Pair Buckhead stops with fewer time-critical errands. Saturday before noon is significantly better than Saturday afternoon.

## School Year Effects
School year weekday mornings add delay near major school clusters in Forsyth, Cobb, and Gwinnett commute sheds. Sequence school-adjacent stops after 9am when feasible. Summer and school holidays normalize these patterns significantly.

## OTP to ITP Crossing Penalty
Crossing from OTP to ITP or vice versa always costs more time than the map distance suggests. OTP north to ITP east is dominated by GA-400 and I-285 geometry. Budget an extra 15 to 25 minutes for any cross-perimeter trip during peak hours.

## I-20 East Corridor
Stone Mountain corridor and I-20 east have distinct rush character from Midtown. Do not apply a single Atlanta traffic model to the whole metro. East side and west side of intown have different arterial congestion patterns even at similar mileage from downtown.

## Airport Corridor
ATL airport runs need explicit terminal, parking, and shuttle buffer time. Never chain an airport errand before a hard appointment elsewhere. Budget a minimum of 90 minutes for any airport stop regardless of apparent distance.

## Multi-County Days
Days with stops in multiple non-adjacent counties benefit from grouping stops within county clusters before long cross-metro jumps. Fulton plus Gwinnett in one trip without clustering is a common sequencing mistake that adds unnecessary driving.
